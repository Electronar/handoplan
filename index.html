D.1 EXECUTIVE OVERVIEW
Purpose of this Document This plan establishes the structured approach for commissioning and handover of the Library Learning Centre upon practical completion. It ensures all building systems operate safely and efficiently, all statutory requirements are met, and the school is fully prepared for occupation.
D.2 COMMISSIONING TIMELINE & MILESTONES
Phase Duration Timing Key Activities
Pre-Commissioning 2 weeks Week 50-52 Systems testing, documentation review, final preparation
Practical Completion — Week 52 Formal sign-off by Principal Contractor, Architect & School
Defects Liability Period 12 months Week 52 – Month 13 Monitoring, defect rectification, interim certificates issued
Snagging Phase Ongoing Week 52 – Month 12 Minor defects identification and rectification
Final Certificate Issue — Month 13 Upon all defects cleared and obligations met

Mechanical Systems

Heating system (boilers, radiators, thermostatic controls)
Ventilation & Heat Recovery Unit (HRU) – Validate 30% heating demand reduction

Main distribution board and sub-circuits – Full inspection
Emergency lighting and exit signage – Functionality test

Plumbing & Water Systems

Water supply pressure and flow rate verification
Low-flow sanitaryware (4.5L/flush maximum) – Flow testing

Drainage systems and grease traps – Clearance and functionality
Legionella risk assessment – Completion and sign-off

IT & Audio-Visual Systems

Network infrastructure – Speed and coverage testing
Wi-Fi connectivity – Campus-wide coverage verification
Smart classroom AV systems – Functionality and training
IT suite servers and workstations – Performance baseline
Security system integration – Access control and alarm testing

Building Envelope & Fire Safety

Curtain walling thermal performance (U-value 0.18 W/m²K) – Verification
Fire-rated elements (60-minute precast planks) – Certification
Green roof and photovoltaic panels (45kWp) – Output verification
Safe working at height systems – Inspection and certification
Fire Risk Assessment – Completion and review
D.4 DEFECTS LIABILITY PERIOD (12 MONTHS)

Monthly Interim Certificates

Issue Authority: Project Architect
Frequency: Monthly throughout the 12-month DLP
Contents:
  • Site inspection findings and observations
  • Defects log and required rectification actions
  • Works completed satisfactorily
  • Contractor compliance status against DLP requirements
  • Certification of interim payments (if applicable)

Snagging Protocol & Response Times

Defect Category Definition Contractor Response Time Completion Target
Minor Defects Repair cost < £500 (paint touch-ups, door adjustments, signage) 48 hours 5 working days
Major Defects Repair cost > £500 (equipment replacement, structural repairs) 5 working days 15-30 days (phased)
Urgent/Safety-Critical Safety risk (fire systems, structural cracks, electrical faults) 24 hours Immediate/Off-site if required

D.7 FINAL CERTIFICATE PROCESS (MONTH 13)

Prerequisites for Final Certificate Issue

All of the following MUST be completed before Final Certificate can be issued:
All defects from DLP rectified and signed off by Architect
All statutory certificates obtained and submitted
All testing and commissioning completed with passing results
All warranties and guarantees registered with manufacturers
All O&M documentation submitted to school
Staff training completed and sign-off obtained
Architect final inspection undertaken – Pass
Contractor final account submitted and agreed

Final Certificate Issuance

Upon completion of all prerequisites, the Architect will issue:
  • FINAL CERTIFICATE – Confirming practical completion and all contractual obligations met
  • This triggers release of final payment to Principal Contractor
  • Defects liability period commences (12 months from practical completion)
  • Full responsibility for maintenance transfers to school after 12-month period
D.8 POST-HANDOVER PERIOD – SCHOOL OCCUPATION

Soft Landings Protocol (First 3 Months of Occupation)

Month 1: Building Performance Monitoring
  • Energy consumption tracking and analysis
  • Temperature and humidity monitoring
  • User feedback collection on comfort levels
  • System fine-tuning (thermostat calibration, ventilation adjustments)
Month 2: Performance Evaluation
  • Compare actual vs. predicted energy use
  • Identify operational issues and rectify
  • Collect staff feedback on building systems
  • Validate BREEAM sustainability targets being met
Month 3: Fine-tuning & Optimization
  • Final adjustments to HVAC, lighting, and controls
  • Staff training updates if required
  • Building performance baseline established
  • Handover of monitoring to school facilities team

Ongoing Post-Handover Responsibilities

Year 1 (Defects Liability Period)
  • Principal Contractor remains responsible for any defects
  • Architect conducts 3-month, 6-month, and final inspections
  • Emergency repairs available 24/7 contact number provided
  • Warranty claims monitored and processed
Year 2 Onwards
  • Full responsibility transferred to school Facilities Manager
  • Maintenance managed under school's facilities contract
  • Quarterly building performance reviews recommended
  • Annual plant servicing (heating, HRU, electrical systems)
  • 5-year major component reviews
D.9 DEFECT DISPUTE RESOLUTION PROCESS

Dispute Escalation Procedure

If school and Contractor disagree on whether item is a defect:
Step Authority Timeframe Action
1 Project Architect 5 working days Independent assessment and determination
2 Mediation (if unresolved) 10 working days Neutral third party facilitated discussion
3 Adjudication (if still unresolved) 28 days Formal dispute resolution – Binding decision

Timeline Extension Protocol

If defects cannot be completed within 12-month DLP:
  • Contractor must provide detailed completion schedule
  • Retention fund held from final payment (typically 5-10% of contract value)
  • Extended DLP period agreed (maximum 2 months additional)
  • Retention fund released upon final completion and sign-off
